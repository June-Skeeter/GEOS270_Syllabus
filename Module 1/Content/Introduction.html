An introduction to Geographic Information Science

What is Geographic Information Science?

The science and study of geospatial information. GIS is a framework we use to understand spatial information. GIS is inherently multidisciplinary with roots in:

Computer Science

Mathematics & Statistics

Cartography

Remote Sensing

What is a Geographic Information System?

A system/tool we use to analyze geospatial information. A GIS can be help solve a wide range of geographic problems in different fields:

Epidemiology/Public Health

Planning (Urban, Hazards)

Conservation & Resource Management

Ecology/Biology

Economics

Climate Science

What will we do with GIS?

Lecture will explain theories and concepts of GIScience and provide more background on GISystems. Labs will show you how to do GIScience using GISystems. Labs will focus on applied examples and practical solutions. By the end of term, you will be able to apply GIS to a problem you care about. You will demonstrate this with your final project
